# Cradle of Humankind

## Category
UNESCO World Heritage Site

## Location
Gauteng Province, South Africa

## Summary
The Cradle of Humankind is one of the world's most significant fossil sites, providing evidence of early human evolution. It contains numerous caves where some of the oldest hominin fossils have been discovered, helping scientists understand the origins of humanity.

## Key Facts

- Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999
- Located northwest of Johannesburg
- Home to the Sterkfontein Caves
- Contains fossils dating back millions of years
- One of the richest hominin fossil sites in the world

## Cultural Significance

The Cradle of Humankind is internationally recognised for its contribution to understanding human origins. It represents South Africa's role in one of humanity's greatest scientific discoveries.
