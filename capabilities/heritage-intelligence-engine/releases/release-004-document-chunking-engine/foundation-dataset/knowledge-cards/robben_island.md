# Robben Island

## Category
Historical Site

## Location
Western Cape, South Africa

## Summary
Robben Island served as a prison for political prisoners during apartheid. It is most famous for imprisoning Nelson Mandela for eighteen years and today stands as a symbol of freedom, resilience, and democracy.

## Key Facts

- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Located off the coast of Cape Town
- Nelson Mandela was imprisoned there for 18 years
- Former maximum-security prison
- Open to visitors as a museum

## Cultural Significance

Robben Island reminds South Africans of the struggle against apartheid and the importance of human rights, democracy, and reconciliation.
