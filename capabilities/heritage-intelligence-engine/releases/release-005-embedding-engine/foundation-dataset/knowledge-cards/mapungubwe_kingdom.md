# Mapungubwe Kingdom

## Category
Heritage Site

## Location
Limpopo Province, South Africa

## Summary
Mapungubwe was one of Southern Africa's earliest advanced kingdoms, flourishing between approximately 1075 and 1220 CE. It became an important centre of trade, leadership, and cultural development.

## Key Facts

- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Located near the borders of Botswana and Zimbabwe
- Famous for the Golden Rhinoceros
- Evidence of international trade with Asia and the Middle East
- Considered one of Southern Africa's earliest kingdoms

## Cultural Significance

Mapungubwe demonstrates the sophistication of early African civilization through governance, trade, architecture, metallurgy, and craftsmanship. It remains an important symbol of South African heritage and identity.
