# Nelson Mandela

## Category
Historical Figure

## Location
Eastern Cape, South Africa

## Summary
Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was an anti-apartheid leader, statesman, and South Africa's first democratically elected President. He dedicated his life to equality, reconciliation, democracy, and nation-building. His leadership helped South Africa transition peacefully from apartheid to democracy, making him one of the most respected leaders in modern history.

## Key Facts

- Born on 18 July 1918 in Mvezo, Eastern Cape
- Imprisoned for 27 years for opposing apartheid
- Released from prison on 11 February 1990
- Became South Africa's first democratically elected President in 1994
- Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 alongside F.W. de Klerk
- Often affectionately known as "Madiba"

## Cultural Significance

Nelson Mandela is recognised globally as a symbol of peace, reconciliation, justice, and servant leadership. His commitment to forgiveness and nation-building helped unite South Africa during one of the country's most significant periods of change. His legacy continues to inspire leaders and communities around the world.
